About me

Robert Primas is a PhD student of Stefan Mangard in the Secure Systems (SESYS) group at the Institute of Applied Information Processing and Communications (IAIK) in Graz, Austria. His research interests include practical aspects of information security such as constructing and attacking cryptographic implementations as well as more theoretic aspects such as designing cryptographic schemes that feature robustness against implementation attacks in addition to cryptanalytic security. He enjoys exploring synergies between cryptanalytic and implementation attacks, as well as building tools that help verify the protection of cryptographic schemes/implementations against various kinds of attacks. He co-designed ISAP, a lightweight authenticated cipher that is currently competing in the NIST lightweight crypto standardization process. He received his MSc in Computer Science at Graz University of Technology with focus on IT Security. His Master's Thesis is about Single-Trace Attacks on Lattice-based Encryption and was supervised by Stefan Mangard and Peter Pessl.
