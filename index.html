About me

I am a PhD student at the Institute of Applied Information Processing and Communications (IAIK) in Graz, Austria and received my MSc in Computer Science at Graz University of Technology with focus on IT Security. My research interests include practical aspects of information security such as constructing and attacking cryptographic implementations as well as more theoretic aspects such as designing cryptographic schemes that feature robustness against physical attacks in addition to cryptanalytic security. I co-designed ISAP, a lightweight authenticated cipher that is currently competing in the NIST lightweight crypto standardization process and gives out-of-the-box strong guarantees against various kinds of physical attacks like fault injection and power analysis.
